# Read My Screen Changelog

## [1.0.0 — Initial release] - 2026-06-20

First stable release of **Read My Screen**: a Raycast-native bridge from what’s on your display—or in your browser—to the model you already trust. Bring your own API keys; every request goes straight from your Mac to **OpenAI**, **Anthropic**, or **Google**—no intermediary backend, no mystery routing.

### Screen, clipboard, and files

- **Read My Screen** (main command): capture a **region**, **window**, or **full screen**, pull a **clipboard image**, or switch to **browser tab** mode—vision models for pixels, text models for page content, with a full **chat** thread and **session history** baked in.
- **Quick: Clipboard Image** and **Quick: Browser Page**: one-shot shortcuts when you already know the input—fast OCR, error triage, or “what does this page say?” without opening the full flow.
- **Analyze Image File**: open **PNG**, **JPEG**, **WebP**, or **GIF** from disk, analyze with your chosen model, and **continue the conversation** in the same chat.

### Models and control

- Pick from **GPT-4o** / **GPT-4o mini**, **GPT-4.1** variants, **Claude Sonnet 4**, **Claude Haiku 4.5**, and **Gemini 2.5** / **2.0**—each gated by the matching **API key** in preferences.
- Tune **default instructions** for new runs, and optionally surface **estimated token usage** and rough **USD cost** when you want visibility into spend.

### History and trust

- **Session History**: revisit past analyses with **thumbnails**, resume chats, and manage stored sessions—your workflow, preserved on device via Raycast storage.
- Keys stay in **Raycast preferences**; you choose what leaves your machine and under which provider’s terms.
